What to include

Use these prompts to make sure you cover the information needed for the assessment report:

  • Condition history – onset, duration, progression, variability, recent changes
  • Medication – names, doses, frequency, effectiveness, side effects
  • Social & occupational history – living situation, routines, work/education, support
  • Daily living – Preparing food; Taking nutrition; Managing therapy; Washing & bathing; Managing toilet needs; Dressing & undressing; Communicating verbally; Reading & understanding; Engaging face to face; Budgeting
  • Mobility – Planning & following journeys; Moving around (distances, aids, stability, pain)
  • Mental state observations – mood, behaviour, cognition, communication, engagement
  • Informal observations – anything noted before/during/after the call not captured elsewhere
  • Reliability – can activities be done safely, to an acceptable standard, repeatedly, and in a reasonable time
  • Fluctuation – typical day, better/worse days, frequency and impact
  • Collateral evidence – key points from documents or third‑party input
